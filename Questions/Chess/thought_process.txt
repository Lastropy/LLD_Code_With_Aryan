Part 1: State Functionalities
- Board: Normally 8x8
- Players: Usually 2
- Pieces: 16 pieces - Rooks (2), Knights (2), bishops(2), queen(1), king(1), pawns (8)
- Pieces have fixed starting position
- Each piece has it's own valid moves it can do.
=> King can move 1 square in any direction
=> Queen can move any number of squares in any direction
=> Bishop can move any number of squares along the 2 diagonals it is part of.
=> Rook can move any number of squares along the row and column it is part of.
=> Pawn can move 2 squares forward in its row, on its first move and 1 square forward in subsequent moves. It captures diagonally.
=> Knight moves in "L" direction, it moves 2 squares in 1 direction and 1 square in perpendicular direction.

- Players take turns to move pieces to valid position, if no other piece in between.
- If you move your piece to a square containing a piece of opposite colour, then the opponent's piece is said to be captured. Your piece stays, their piece goes away.
- If a king is at a position reachable by an opp color piece, king is in check.
- If a king is in check and none of its colour pieces can capture the piece or block it, the king is said to be in checkmate.
- Checkmating the king is the objective of the game.
- If the opp colour piece has no valid moves left and the king is not in check, then it is called stalemate (draw).
- Game goes on until either checkmate happens or stalemate happens.
